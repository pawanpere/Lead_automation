# Cold Email Frameworks — TikTok Shop x Ecommerce Brands

Three frameworks adapted for pitching TikTok Shop services to ecommerce brands across all categories (beauty, skincare, fashion, supplements, home, fitness, food & beverage, pet, electronics, etc.).

---

## Framework 1: Brand Love → Opportunity → Proof → Ask (BOPA)

**Best for:** When you've genuinely looked at their brand/products and can lead with specific appreciation. This is your DEFAULT framework.

### Structure
```
[Brand Love]:    Something specific you love about their brand/products
[Opportunity]:   How TikTok Shop connects to what they're already doing
[Proof]:         One concrete result from a similar brand
[Ask]:           Low-friction question
```

### How to Write Each Part

**Brand Love** — Must be SPECIFIC. Name actual products, features, or brand values. Adapt to whatever category they're in.
- Bad: "I love your brand!"
- Good (beauty): "I'm absolutely in love with your clean makeup essentials and how you combine performance with conscious ingredients."
- Good (fitness): "Your adjustable dumbbells are genius — the space-saving design is exactly what apartment gym people need."
- Good (pet): "I spent way too long on your site — the grain-free treat line is exactly the kind of thing my dog would go crazy for."
- Good (fashion): "Your linen collection is fire — the oversized fits with that washed texture is exactly what's trending right now."

**Opportunity** — Connect TikTok Shop to their world. Don't explain what TikTok Shop is — show why it fits THEM and their specific category.
- Bad: "TikTok Shop is a great platform for selling products."
- Good (beauty): "Brands like yours are perfect for creator-led content — people want to see how clean beauty products actually perform in real routines."
- Good (supplements): "Wellness brands crush it on TikTok Shop — the 'what I take every morning' format is one of the highest-converting content types on the platform."
- Good (home): "Home products go viral on TikTok constantly — the before/after and unboxing content practically makes itself."

**Proof** — One result. Keep it tight. Make it relevant to their size/category.
- Bad: "We've worked with hundreds of brands."
- Good: "We helped a similar brand in your space go from zero to 300+ daily orders through creator content in under 8 weeks."

**Ask** — One question. Easy to say yes to.
- Bad: "Can we schedule a 45-minute strategy session?"
- Good: "Would you be interested in hearing more details?"

### Full Example

**Subject:** tiktok shop opportunity - On Lo Beauty

> Hi Diana, hope you're having a stellar Friday.
>
> This is still your website, right?
>
> I'm absolutely in love with On Lo Beauty's clean makeup essentials and skin friendly formulations and how they combine performance with conscious ingredients.
>
> I help beauty/skincare brands turn TikTok into a real sales channel through TikTok Shop. I've worked with similar beauty/skincare brands to generate millions of new impressions within weeks, and hundreds of daily orders through creator-led content, where thousands of creators show how the product fits into their routines, and viewers can check out instantly. All at the cost of the samples.
>
> We handle the entire platform for you, end-to-end, and are confident we can take Onlo to the top of its category within months.
>
> Would love to explore this opportunity further with you! Even if this isn't on your radar, we can use this time as a discovery conversation about TikTok Shop to point you in the right direction.
>
> Would you be interested in hearing more details?
>
> Warmly,
> Sayim Khan
>
> P.S. ~ also, we don't identify as a traditional agency, so no tossing the work out to crappy account managers. We're a 2-man team who get the job done ourselves completely :)

## Framework 2: Question → Insight → Ask (QIA)

**Best for:** When you don't have a strong specific hook about their brand but know their category well. Good for batch outreach to any ecommerce vertical.

### Structure
```
[Question]:  Something they're probably already thinking about
[Insight]:   A non-obvious observation about their category on TikTok
[Ask]:       Low-friction CTA
```

### Full Example

**Subject:** quick question about [Brand]

> Hey [Name],
>
> Quick question — has TikTok Shop come up in any of your growth conversations yet?
>
> I ask because [their category] is one of the fastest-moving categories on the platform right now, and the brands getting in early are seeing insane organic reach through creator content — we're talking millions of impressions just from sending out samples.
>
> The ones who wait usually end up competing with 10x more brands for the same creator pool.
>
> Happy to share what's working in your category right now — 15 minutes, no pitch, just useful context.
>
> [Name]
>
> P.S. ~ we only work with 3-4 brands at a time. Not a pitch — just context for why I'm reaching out now vs. later.

## Framework 3: Trigger → Insight → Ask (TIA)

**Best for:** When you spot a specific trigger — they just launched a new product, got press coverage, went viral on social, products are sold out, or they're clearly scaling.

### Structure
```
[Trigger]:   Name the specific thing you noticed
[Insight]:   What that means for their TikTok opportunity
[Ask]:       Time-aware, low-friction request
```

### Full Example

**Subject:** your new launch

> Hey [Name],
>
> Saw you just dropped [new product] — congrats, it looks incredible.
>
> New launches are the perfect TikTok Shop moment. When creators organically show a brand-new product in their content and viewers can buy it right there in the video, the conversion rates are wild. We've seen new product launches do 2-3x the volume of evergreen SKUs in the first two weeks on the platform.
>
> Would it be worth a quick chat about getting [product] in front of creators while the launch energy is still hot?
>
> [Name]

## Choosing the Right Framework

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| You've browsed their site, love specific products | BOPA (default) |
| Batch outreach, know the category well | QIA |
| New product launch, press, sold-out products, viral moment | TIA |
| Products are sold out on their site | TIA (sold-out = demand signal) |
| They're already on TikTok but not on TikTok Shop | BOPA with "you're already creating content" angle |
